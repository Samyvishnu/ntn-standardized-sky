# ntn-standardized-sky
🛰️ 85% of Earth has no cell signal. 3GPP Release 17 just changed the rules.
🛰️ I spent 18 years managing telecom programs. Then I built an infographic to explain why the next 5 years will be different from everything before.
In 5G NR NTN, 3GPP did something that sounds simple but took years of standards work to achieve:
They made satellite connectivity speak the same language as your smartphone.
Same protocol. Same chipset. No special device.
A phone in a dead zone — ocean, desert, mountain — just switches from a cell tower to a satellite and keeps going. The user sees nothing change.
That's not science fiction. That's Release 17. That's commercially live today.
I created this infographic to break down the 6 architectural breakthroughs behind the standardized sky — from Extended HARQ Timing to cloud-native vRAN to Doppler pre-compensation baked into chipsets you already carry.
If you work in telecom, satellite, IoT, or network program management — this is the architectural shift that will define the next decade of connectivity infrastructure.
The coverage gap is 85% of Earth's surface.
The standard to close it already exists.
The race now is execution.
